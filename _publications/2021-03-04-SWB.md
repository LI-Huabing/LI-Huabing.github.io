There has been considerable attention in the relationship between socioeconomic class and subjective well-being. Using the Chinese General Social Survey (CGSS) 2018 data and the Ordered Probit Regression model, this paper examines the effects of class affiliation and class mobility on people’s subjective well-being, discovering that: First, both class affiliation and class mobility have a significant positive influence on subjective well-being; Second, subjective class affiliation has the greatest impact on subjective well-being, followed by subjective class mobility, objective class affiliation, and objective class mobility. The findings show that telling the lively Chinese story, meeting people’s aspirations for a better life, solidly promoting common prosperity, and appropriately maintaining a reasonable economic growth rate in the future are practically significant for improving people’s subjective well-being.
