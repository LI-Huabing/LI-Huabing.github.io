Building a “Community of Shared Future for Mankind” (CSFM) is an unprecedented initiative for China to shoulder active responsibility and improve global governance as a major emerging power, in which process the “Belt and Road Initiative” (BRI) is the first practiced and the most anticipated exploration. The article is dedicated to investigating whether the policy shocks of the BRI have led to a higher level of the CSFM for the countries along the route using the “five connection” data and the difference-in-difference approach, through tackling the endogeneity problem by propensity score matching. The empirical results indicate that the BRI has significantly contributed to enhancing the note countries’ level of the CSFM. In the coming stage, we should continue to promote the construction of the “five connection” of the BRI, especially the construction of the people-to-people connection, in order to promote the implementation of the CSFM.
